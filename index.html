About

Where signal processing
meets the atmosphere.

I'm a master's student at KAIST Aerospace Engineering, working in the Navigation & GPS Lab under Prof. Jiyun Lee. My research focuses on GNSS Radio Occultation — a remote sensing technique that uses signals from navigation satellites to probe Earth's atmosphere.

The lower troposphere is where RO is hardest: multipath propagation, sharp moisture gradients, and super-refraction corrupt the signal. I work on algorithms — wavelet-based phase matching and local spectral width estimation — that push reliable bending angle retrieval closer to the surface.

When I'm not processing GNSS data, I'm usually hiking — which doubles as fieldwork for mountain-based RO signal collection.

Research

Current Projects

01 — WTPM

Wavelet-Transform Phase Matching

Extending Phase Matching with Synchrosqueezed Wavelet Transform (SST) to sharpen time-frequency resolution and suppress cross-term interference, enabling multi-ray extraction in heavily multipathed occultations.

SSTPhase MatchingMulti-ridgeViterbi

02 — LSW

Local Spectral Width Algorithm

STFT-based spectral width estimator for automatically detecting multipath regions in bending angle profiles. Uses CDF-threshold crossing on a 0.5 km altitude grid for robust, reproducible flagging.

STFTSpectral WidthCDF ThresholdLiu 2017

03 — KPS

KPS Ionospheric Correction

NeQuick-G Az parameter optimization for the Korean Positioning System with daily, hourly, and grid-based strategies. Validated against GPS Klobuchar and Galileo NeQuick-G over the Korean Peninsula.
